## 1. Summary

RFQ for buildings 1475 and 1479 at JBSA-Fort Sam Houston: **Lift Beds (786),
Bunkable Beds (357), Wardrobes (438), and Bed Stacking Kits (357)**. A recurring
AETC/JBSA furniture buy (2020 precedent `FA301620-RAA0143`, same "beds &
wardrobes for JBSA-Ft. Sam Houston" requirement). Surfaced via the logged-in
BidNet six-state bed/bedding search and promoted to the active pipeline as an
explicit "bed" product opportunity pending a fit/spec review. This memo is that
review.

## 2. Disqualifying factors

- **Primary (product mismatch) - no bedding fit:** The package is institutional
  metal bed **frames** (lift beds, bunkable beds, bed stacking kits) plus
  **wardrobes** (casegoods). There are **no mattresses or bedding** in it.
  Continental manufactures mattresses, box springs, and basic bed frames - it
  does **not** make wardrobes, bunkable/lift beds, or stacking kits. This is a
  furniture/casegoods buy, structurally outside Continental's product line; the
  "bed" keyword that promoted it is not a bedding fit.
- **Cannot submit:** The full SAM.gov entity registration is not Active
  (UEI `XF73FG8CVMX1` assigned; Financial/EFT still pending). A federal offer
  due **2026-07-01** is not submittable, and even an Active-today entity would
  not clear IRS TIN match + CAGE assignment in time.
- **Specs inaccessible without cost/contact:** The full SOW sits behind BidNet's
  **$748/yr** Texas Federal paywall, or a call to 802 CONS contracting. The
  solicitation is **not indexed** on SAM.gov public search or HigherGov as of
  review (only the 2020 precedent is). Free-source research is exhausted short of
  operator phone calls.

## 3. What would change a future bid/no-bid call

- A **CONUS DoD barracks/dorm solicitation that includes a mattress / bedding
  CLIN** (this one has none) once SAM is **Active** is the genuine fit.
- The recurring AETC/MICC barracks furniture channel is already tracked in
  `leads/review/_lead_radar.csv`
  (`sam-gov-federal-...-barracks-billeting-...-fa301626q0151`) - watch there for
  the **next** cycle, and revisit the BidNet $748 upgrade deliberately (not
  under deadline) if a future cycle carries a real bedding CLIN.

## 4. Lessons captured

- A **"beds" keyword match is not a bedding product fit.** Lift/bunkable beds +
  wardrobes are institutional furniture, not Continental's line. The hard-rule
  product-fit check reclassifies this from active pipeline to a Lead Radar
  channel watch.
- Treat AETC/MICC barracks **furniture-set** buys as recurring **channel
  watches** (Lead Radar), not active bids, unless an explicit mattress/bedding
  CLIN is present.
