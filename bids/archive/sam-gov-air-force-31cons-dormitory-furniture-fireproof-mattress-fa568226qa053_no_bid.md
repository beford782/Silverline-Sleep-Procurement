## 1. Summary

Updated SAM.gov Combined Synopsis/Solicitation for the **supply, delivery,
installation, and removal of 102 sets of dormitory furniture**, with
Amendment #1 adding a unit quantity to **CLIN #10 (Fireproof Mattress)** on
Attachment 1. Offers due 2026-07-20. Surfaced by a SAM.gov sweep and promoted
to `watching` because it is a current open dormitory-furniture solicitation
with an explicit mattress CLIN; held at `high` risk pending an
attachment/location review. This memo is that review.

The buyer, **31 CONS**, is the 31st Contracting Squadron at **Aviano Air Base,
Italy** (31st Fighter Wing; supports the wing and ~26 geographically separated
units across Southern Europe). Confirmed via the Aviano AB / 31st Contracting
Squadron public fact sheet, 2026-06-25.

## 2. Disqualifying factors

- **Primary (geography) - OCONUS:** Delivery is to Aviano Air Base, Italy.
  Continental's service geography is TX/OK/LA/MS/AR/NM and delivery is on its
  **own fleet (dock delivery)**. There is no international freight, customs, or
  in-theater logistics capability. This alone is disqualifying.
- **Primary (scope) - not a mattress buy:** The requirement is complete
  dormitory furniture **sets** plus **on-site installation and removal**.
  Continental supplies mattresses, bed frames, and box springs; it does **not**
  offer installation or removal/disposal (documented capability gaps in the
  vendor profile) and does not manufacture the case goods / wardrobes / desks /
  bunk beds that make up a furniture "set." The fireproof-mattress **CLIN #10**
  is the only product-fit line, and it cannot be bid in isolation from an
  all-or-nothing 102-set package that also requires install + removal abroad.
- **Not submittable yet:** The full SAM.gov All Awards entity registration is
  not Active (UEI `XF73FG8CVMX1` assigned; Financial/EFT banking still
  outstanding), so no federal offer can be submitted regardless of fit.
- **Contributing:** OCONUS USAFE furnishing buys typically carry SOFA / base
  access, Italian VAT / tax-relief, DFARS overseas clauses, and in-country
  delivery/install obligations that structurally favor in-country / EU vendors
  or firms with established OCONUS logistics.

## 3. What would change a future bid/no-bid call

- A **CONUS (domestic)** Air Force / DoD dormitory, barracks, or correctional
  **mattress** solicitation in NAICS 337910 / PSC 7210 with dock delivery and
  **no installation/removal** is the genuine fit - pursue those once SAM is
  Active.
- Overseas, set-based FF&E packages with installation are out of scope
  **structurally**, not just on timing: completing SAM registration would not
  make this one winnable. Treat OCONUS contracting squadrons (31 CONS and other
  USAFE/PACAF offices) as geography no-fit at triage.

## 4. Lessons captured

- The funnel correctly surfaced a real "dormitory furniture + fireproof
  mattress" signal - not a false positive - and the `high` risk score was
  warranted. The attachment/location review is what converts it from a
  product-keyword match to a clear **structural** no-bid (OCONUS + install +
  set-package), distinct from the SAM-not-Active **timing** no-bids (e.g. USCG
  Base Boston).
- Add an early triage check on the **buyer's location**: an Air Force
  contracting squadron number (e.g. "31 CONS") resolves to a specific base, and
  for overseas wings that is a fast no-fit signal before any attachment work.
