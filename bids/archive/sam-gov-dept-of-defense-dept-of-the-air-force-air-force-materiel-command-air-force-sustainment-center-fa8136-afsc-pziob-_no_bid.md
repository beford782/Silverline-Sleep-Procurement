## 1. Summary

Sources Sought (market research) for dormitory mattresses at the Tinker
AFB fire station, S Coffeyville, OK (NAICS 337910 / PSC 7210), response
due 2026-06-17. Initial review showed strong product/geography fit and
the gating SAM.gov-registration prerequisite was confirmed active, so the
working decision was to respond with a capability statement.

On closer reading of the requirement, the notice specifies **Purple-brand
mattresses**. No-bid.

## 2. Disqualifying factor

- **Primary reason:** The notice calls out Purple-brand mattresses
  specifically. Continental does not manufacture the Purple brand; its
  brands are Restonic / Spring Air / Silverline Sleep. Continental cannot
  represent itself as able to furnish the named brand.

## 3. What would change a future bid/no-bid call

- A solicitation written "brand name **or equal**" rather than
  Purple-specific, allowing Continental's institutional dormitory line to
  compete on an equivalent basis.
- A separate Tinker AFB / AFSC requirement for institutional dormitory
  mattresses without a single-brand specification.

## 4. Lessons captured

- Confirm any **brand-name specification** before investing in a
  capability statement; product-category fit (dormitory + fire-retardant,
  NAICS 337910) is not enough when a specific brand is named.
- A brand-name notice is a reliable no-bid even at perfect NAICS match and
  in-geography delivery. Watch for a future "or equal" or unbranded
  follow-on solicitation.
