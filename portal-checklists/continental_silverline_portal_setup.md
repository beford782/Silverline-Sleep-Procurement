# Step 2: Portal Registration and Commodity-Code Checklist

This checklist is the next operational step for Continental Silverline after completing the vendor profile. The goal is to make sure the company receives the right mattress, bedding, furniture, dormitory, correctional, medical, shelter, and emergency-response bid notifications.

## 1. Highest-Priority Portal Actions

| Priority | Portal / Buyer Channel | Current Status | Action | Why It Matters |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Harris County Bonfire | Not registered | Create a free Bonfire vendor account and select every relevant commodity category. | Harris County and related departments are a high-value local target for jail, public health, facility, and emergency needs. |
| 2 | Houston ISD IonWave | Not registered | Register in IonWave and select mattress, bedding, furniture, dormitory, and institutional product commodities. | HISD uses IonWave for prospective suppliers and sends email notifications based on selected commodities. |
| 3 | Texas CMBL | Registered | Audit and update NIGP codes, contact email, service regions, and profile completeness. | Texas agencies use CMBL profiles and NIGP commodity codes to identify vendors for state bid opportunities. |
| 4 | Texas ESBD / Texas SmartBuy | Active | Build a weekly saved-search routine for mattress and related terms. | ESBD is the key state opportunity board for formal public postings. |
| 5 | City of Houston Beacon Bid | Active | Confirm subscription status, commodity categories, and notification settings. | City of Houston uses Beacon Bid for electronic bidding, questions, answers, and supplier notifications. |
| 6 | University portals | Interested | Register first with UH, UT System/UT Austin, Texas A&M System, Texas Southern University, and nearby community colleges. | Universities and community colleges are high-fit buyers for dormitory, residence hall, and facilities replacement opportunities. |
| 7 | Cooperatives | Interested | Monitor BuyBoard, Choice Partners, TIPS, HGACBuy, Sourcewell, and OMNIA for relevant furniture, mattress, dormitory, or institutional-supply solicitations. | Cooperative contracts can make it easier for public entities to buy after award. |

## 2. Portal Setup Notes From Official Sources

- Texas CMBL is the state vendor database used by Texas purchasing entities to build vendor lists based on products and services offered. Active vendors should keep the CMBL profile current, including the email address used for bid notifications.
- Texas CMBL vendors can modify NIGP commodity codes and contact information in their CMBL eSystems account.
- City of Houston suppliers still register through SAP Business Network, then use Beacon Bid to review/respond to solicitations, subscribe for notifications, follow solicitations, submit questions, and receive answers.
- Houston ISD directs prospective suppliers to register in IonWave and notes that email notifications are tied to selected product/service commodities.
- Harris County vendor registration routes suppliers through Bonfire; vendors should create a free Bonfire account under the new vendor registration path.

## 5. Portal Registration Checklist

For each portal, capture the following in a private working tracker:

- Portal name
- Registration URL
- Username / login owner
- Vendor ID or registration number
- Commodity categories selected
- Notification email address
- Renewal date, if applicable
- Documents uploaded
- Insurance documents uploaded, if required
- W-9 uploaded, if required
- Conflict-of-interest or vendor forms completed
- Notes on how to submit questions
- Notes on how to submit bids
